## Goal for this iteration

`N % 10 == 0` — refactor iteration. Surveyed `apps/sidecar/src/`
and noticed the highest-leverage cleanup: `server.ts` and
`persistence.ts` both call `listProjectFiles(blobStore, id)` once
per WS connection — persistence does it during hydration to seed
`knownFiles`, and the connection handler does it again right
after `awaitHydrated()` to build the `file-list` control frame.
Iter 58 flagged this as deduplicable but punted on the grounds
that the two sets are conceptually distinct. After iter 59 they
no longer are: `knownFiles` IS the set of files the sidecar
operates on, and the `file-list` message is meant to expose
exactly that set to the client. Two reads of the same blob
listing also risks divergence on eventually-consistent stores.

## Approach

- Add `files(): string[]` to `ProjectPersistence`. Always returns
  the sorted union of `MAIN_DOC_NAME` plus every blob discovered
  during hydration; degrades to `[MAIN_DOC_NAME]` cleanly when
  hydration failed or no blob store is configured. Must be called
  after `awaitHydrated()` for a complete view.
- Pre-seed `knownFiles` with `MAIN_DOC_NAME` at construction time
  (rather than at the end of the hydration success path) so the
  failure path naturally exposes `["main.tex"]`. Drop the now-
  redundant `knownFiles.add(MAIN_DOC_NAME)` inside the hydration
  block.
- In `server.ts`, replace the inline `listProjectFiles` call
  (and its try/catch + warn) with `project.persistence.files()`.
  Drop the `listProjectFiles` import.

## Actions

- `apps/sidecar/src/persistence.ts`: `ProjectPersistence.files()`

  seeds `knownFiles` at construction and exposes a sorted view.
- `apps/sidecar/src/server.ts`: import trimmed; `file-list`
  payload now comes from `project.persistence.files()`; the inline

  already covers the failure mode, and the fallback degrades

## Notes for future iterations

- `listProjectFiles` is still a separate exported primitive
  (tested in `listProjectFiles.test.mjs`) — it's the low-level
  blob-store listing helper. Persistence owns the in-memory
  `knownFiles` state; the helper still has a place for code paths
  that need a fresh read (e.g. when file-tree CRUD lands and we
  need to re-list before re-emitting `file-list`).
- The `file-list` frame is currently sent exactly once per
  connection. When create/rename/delete lands, the natural
  approach is to mutate `knownFiles` in persistence and re-emit
  `files()` to every viewer; the seam now in place makes that
  one-line.
