## Goal for this iteration

Execute the next probe queued by iter 217: drive `supertex --daemon`
directly with rapid back-to-back source-file writes (no intervening
stdin `recompile` commands) to see whether the daemon's file-watcher
re-enters and trips an assert. This was the surprise observation
from iter 217 (`supertex: edit detected at …/main.tex:NN` lines on
stderr — the daemon watches the source file in addition to its
stdin protocol), which re-opens the iter-213-era write/recompile
race hypothesis for GT-7.

## Approach

New gold case `test_supertex_filewatcher_race` driving the real
ELF directly (not via `SupertexDaemonCompiler`, since that always
brackets writes with stdin commands). Two probes:
1. **Pure watcher re-entry.** After daemon ready, write `main.tex`
   10 times in a tight loop (different content each time, brief
   yields), with no stdin commands at all. Watch stderr for any
   `assert` / `Abort` / `SIGABRT`-style failure and the process for
   unexpected exit. After the writes, issue a single `recompile,1`
   and assert the daemon still completes a round normally.
2. **Write→recompile race.** 10 iterations of: write a fresh
   variant of `main.tex`, then *immediately* (no `await`) send
   `recompile,1\n` on stdin. The file-watcher may already have
   scheduled a round by the time the stdin command lands. Assert
   the daemon survives the whole sequence (no protocol violation,
   no child exit, all 10 rounds reach `[round-done]`).

## Findings

- **Pure-watcher-reentry hypothesis is wrong.** 10 rapid rewrites
  of `main.tex` with no intervening stdin command did not crash the
  daemon. The OS file-watcher coalesces them (only a single
  `edit detected` event surfaces on stderr after all 10 writes are
  done), and the daemon stays idle and ready until the next stdin
  `recompile` arrives.
- **Write→recompile-race hypothesis is wrong.** 10 iterations of
  `writeFile(main.tex); writeStdin("recompile,1\n")` in the same
  microtask all complete with `[round-done]`. The daemon does
  surface `[rollback 1]` for the first few iterations (resuming
  from the unchanged page-one checkpoint), then begins emitting
  `supertex: WARN no usable rollback target … (checkpoint=1
  resumed_pid=-1)` followed by an immediate `[round-done]` —
  i.e. the round becomes a *no-op* rather than crashing. This is
  the same upstream rollback-target-missing path that bit us in
  iter 188 (`edit→preview` regression) and motivated the
  no-segments / refuse-to-synthesize fix.
- **New unexpected stderr marker:** `supertex: watching (daemon
  mode; stdin event-loop)`. Suggests the file-watcher and the
  stdin event loop share a single main loop, so a write *after*
  the daemon has consumed a stdin command but *before* it has
  begun the round may or may not be observed — but in our probe
  this never crashes.

The combined picture from iter 217 + 218: every locally-runnable
shape we've tried (oversize T, large-paste, pure rapid rewrites,
write-immediately-before-recompile) is benign. The GT-7 crash
signature must require something the local probes don't cover —
plausible candidates: (a) a specific edit shape from the Yjs path
(non-ASCII, multi-line, mid-token edits the local fixture doesn't
exercise), (b) the macOS/Linux containerised filesystem on Fly
behaving differently from the local tmpdir, (c) a sidecar-side
timing where `runCompile` writes `main.tex` *while a previous
round is still in flight* (the sidecar coalescer is supposed to
prevent this, but iter 200's extraction may need a re-audit), or
(d) sustained load over many keystrokes accumulating state that a
short probe doesn't reach.

## Notes for future iterations

- **Next probe candidate (sidecar audit, not upstream).** Re-read
  `apps/sidecar/src/compileCoalescer.ts` and `runCompile` in
  `apps/sidecar/src/server.ts` for any path that can re-enter
  `writeMain(source)` while a previous `recompile,T` is still
  awaiting `[round-done]`. The upstream daemon won't crash on
  *coalesced* writes, but it might if the sidecar truly issues a
  second `recompile,T` before round-done — which is what would
  produce a `protocol violation` flavour message.
- **Direct-browser repro outstanding.** PLAN's M9.editor-ux.regress.gt7
  step 1 (reproduce the crash manually in a real browser by
  pasting `\newpage X` lines, capture cursor + page count + WS
  trace) is still the right next step now that all the
  local-daemon probes have come back clean. The crash is not in
  the daemon's stdin/file-watch surface; it's somewhere in the
  sidecar→daemon glue or in a sequence we haven't reproduced.
- Both probes stay in the tree as regression-locks on
  watcher-tolerance and write/recompile-race tolerance.
