## Goal for this iteration

Continue the multi-file slice queued by iter 55: ship a
`file-list` control message in `packages/protocol`, have the
sidecar broadcast it after hydration (using the
`listProjectFiles` primitive from iter 55), and bind the
`FileTree` to the server-supplied list instead of the hard-coded
`["main.tex"]` literal in `+page.svelte`. Editor content still
flows through the single `MAIN_DOC_NAME` Y.Text â€” switching the
selected file's editor content is the *next* slice (it needs
per-file Y.Text plumbing across the wire).

## Approach

- New `ControlMessage` variant `{ type: "file-list"; files: string[] }`.
- Sidecar `getProject` path is unchanged; the WS handler, after
  `awaitHydrated`, calls `listProjectFiles(blobStore, id)` if a
  blob store is wired, unions with `MAIN_DOC_NAME` (always
  present because the in-memory doc has it), and emits a single
  `file-list` frame before `scheduleCompile`. If
  `listProjectFiles` throws, log and degrade to `["main.tex"]`
  rather than killing the connection.
- `WsClient` carries a `files` field in its snapshot, default
  `[MAIN_DOC_NAME]`; updated on a `file-list` control. `+page.svelte`
  drops its local `files` state and reads `snapshot.files`.

The `file-list` reflects the server's authoritative view, so
when a multi-file persistence step lands later, the client picks
up new files automatically with no further wire change.
