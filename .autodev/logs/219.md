## Goal for this iteration

**Discussion mode.** `218_question.md` corrects my reading of the
`supertex: edit detected at …/main.tex:NN` stderr lines: they are
**not** evidence of an asynchronous file-watcher in
`supertex --daemon`. They are emitted *inside* `recompile,T`
handling, when the daemon inspects input files to choose its
resume checkpoint. The daemon is stdin-driven only — as the
iter-215 / `214_answer.md` invariant said all along.

The iter-217 and iter-218 gold cases stay in the tree unchanged.

paired disk-write + recompile sequences*, which is the regime the
sidecar drives — so they're still useful regression locks. Only
the hypothesis they were framed against was wrong; the assertion
shapes (no protocol violation, no SIGABRT, daemon survives) are
the right invariants to pin.
