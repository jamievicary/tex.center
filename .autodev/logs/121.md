## Goal for this iteration

Land the prerequisite for M7.5.5: fix the 0/1-indexing mismatch
between `SupertexDaemonCompiler` and the upstream `supertex
--daemon DIR` protocol. PLAN nominally pointed at M7.4.2 (an
upstream supertex serialise/restore wire), but that's a separate
repo with its own autodev loop and a much bigger lift than fits
here; the listed smaller alternative is M7.5.5, and probing it
exposed an actual bug.

## Approach

Manual smoke against `vendor/supertex/build/supertex --daemon`
on a 3-page LaTeX fixture failed with `missing chunk 0.out`. The
upstream protocol from discussion 71 is explicit: chunks are
`1.out`, `2.out`, â€¦ (1-indexed); the daemon source
(`vendor/supertex/tools/supertex_daemon.c:1091`) confirms it
writes `%ld.out` for 1-based LaTeX page numbers.
`SupertexDaemonCompiler.assembleSegment` (and the fake daemon
in the unit test) were 0-indexed, so the assembler asked for
`0.out` which never exists. Single-line fix in the compiler;
fake daemon + assertions follow.

Deliberately **not** in this slice: a standing gold test that
drives the real ELF end-to-end. That's its own coherent piece
(spawn the binary, depend on `vendor/supertex/build/supertex`
being present + system lualatex, decide on flake-tolerance
around the upstream `chunk_writer: invalid range` warning).
Folding it in here would over-bundle. Smoke covered manually
across three deterministic runs: 121 KB `%PDF` segment.
