## Approach

PLAN priority #1 iter A: thread the new upstream `[pdf-end]`
daemon signal (supertex `aaa625a`, on `vendor/supertex`) through
the sidecar's daemon-protocol parser into a per-round
`lastPageReached: boolean`, surface it on `CompileSuccess.lastPage`
and on the assembled `PdfSegment.lastPage`, and widen the protocol
wire-format to carry it as a single tri-state uint8 at the tail of
the `PdfSegment` header (header width 17 → 18 bytes). No FE
consumer in this iteration — iter B (next ordinary iter) lands
the `PdfViewer.svelte` placeholder / `maxViewingPage` swap.

**Wire encoding** (chosen for roundtrip safety):
- `0` = unset (compiler does not expose the signal — e.g.
  `FixtureCompiler`),
- `1` = false (round completed without `[pdf-end]`; more pages may
  follow on a re-compile with a higher target),
- `2` = true (round observed `[pdf-end]`; engine reached
  `\enddocument` and no further shipouts will follow).

A simpler 2-state encoding (`0` = unset/false, `1` = true) would
have collapsed "false" and "undefined" on the wire, losing the
distinction iter B will read to decide whether to spawn a
placeholder slot for page N+1. The tri-state byte is forward-
compatible and one extra byte over the 2-state alternative — cheap
on a per-segment basis.

**Stamping happens at the compiler boundary, not server.ts.** The
PLAN's "sidecar stamps `lastPage` on broadcast segment" is
literally satisfied by `SupertexDaemonCompiler.compile()` setting
`segment.lastPage = events.lastPageReached` before returning —
parallel to its existing `segment.shipoutPage = events.maxShipout`
stamp. `server.ts:runCompile` is unchanged: it broadcasts whatever
the compiler hands back via `encodePdfSegment(seg)`. If a future
iteration moves to multi-segment-per-round, only the last segment
would carry `lastPage=true` and the others `false` — the
mechanism extends cleanly.
