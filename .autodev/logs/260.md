## Goal for this iteration

N=260 — refactor iteration (`N % 10 == 0`). Survey turned up one
clear duplicated-template smell: `e instanceof Error ? e.message :
String(e)` appears verbatim 28 times across `apps/sidecar/src/`,
`apps/web/src/lib/`, and the SvelteKit auth routes. Two files
already define a private `errorMessage(err)` helper of exactly
that shape (`oauthCallback.ts:294`, `testOauthCallback.ts:113`),
which proves the duplication has been felt locally. Consolidate
into one tiny per-app helper module and replace every site.

Per-app rather than a single shared module: `apps/sidecar` and
`apps/web` don't share a "common utils" package today, and
inventing one for a one-liner would be premature
infrastructure. The duplicate one-liner across the two `errors.ts`
files is acceptable cost vs. a new package.

## Approach

1. Add `apps/sidecar/src/errors.ts` and `apps/web/src/lib/errors.ts`,
   each exporting a single `errorMessage(e: unknown): string` that
   inlines the original ternary.
2. Replace every `e instanceof Error ? e.message : String(e)` site
   (and the `err`/`lastErr` variants) with a call to the helper,
   importing from the per-app module.
3. Delete the two pre-existing private `errorMessage` definitions
   in `oauthCallback.ts` / `testOauthCallback.ts`; their callsites
   continue to work against the imported helper (same signature
   and name).

Files touched (replacements only; no behaviour change):

- sidecar: `compiler/fixture.ts`, `compiler/supertexDaemon.ts`,
  `compiler/supertexOnce.ts`, `persistence.ts` (×7), `server.ts` (×6).
- web: `lib/server/oauthCallback.ts` (×3 callsites + helper removed),
  `lib/server/testOauthCallback.ts` (×1 + helper removed),
  `lib/server/readyz.ts`, `lib/server/upstreamResolver.ts`,
  `lib/server/wsProxy.ts` (×6), `lib/wsClient.ts`,
  `routes/auth/google/{callback,start,test-callback}/+server.ts`.

`tests_gold/lib/src/sweepOrphanedSidecarMachines.ts` also carries
the pattern but lives under the gold-suite test infra; out of
scope for this refactor (it doesn't import from either app's
`errors.ts` and consolidating across that boundary is bigger
churn than the refactor warrants).
