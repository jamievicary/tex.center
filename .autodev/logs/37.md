## Decisions

- **`createSession` over `mintSid` + side-channel.** The pure
  orchestrator already had to pass `claims` to the allowlist
  gate; threading them on into the session-mint callback is one
  extra parameter for the wiring layer, and means the route
  binding has everything it needs (`sub`, `email`, `name`) to do
  the user-upsert without re-parsing the ID token. Tests stay
  simple — base input's stub asserts the right `claims.sub` is
  passed through.
- **DB failure → 500.** A persistent DB outage is the right place
  to surface a user-visible error; falling through to a 302 with
  a session cookie whose row doesn't exist would just make
  M5.1.3's hooks lookup misbehave. The state cookie is cleared on
  this branch too — the user can retry sign-in cleanly once the
  DB is back.
- **Singleton, not Fastify-style decoration.** SvelteKit
  `+server.ts` routes don't have a `request.server` of their own
  to hang state on; the canonical pattern is a module-scope
  singleton. The lazy `getDb()` keeps tests that never touch the
  DB free of `DATABASE_URL` requirements.
- **PGlite test in tests_gold, not tests_normal.** Matches the
  existing convention from `test_pglite_migrations`. ~3s of boot
  + query work; tests_normal's 22 cases are unit-fast.
- **`displayName` nullable, refreshed every login.** Google's
  `name` claim is nullable; carrying it through verbatim (including
  re-setting to `null` if the user clears it from their Google
  account) is least surprising. The upsert always rewrites email
  + displayName so a future user-merge or email change lands on
  the next sign-in.
- **`PostgresJsDatabase<Schema>` as the helper's param type.**
  Strongly-typed against prod; PGlite tests cast through `any`.
  Defining a heavyweight cross-driver alias bought nothing —
  Drizzle's query builder is structurally compatible across
  drivers at runtime, and `any` in test wiring is fine.
