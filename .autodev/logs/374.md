## Goal for this iteration

PLAN priority #1 iter B′ — toast-text format slice. Now that iter B
(landed iter 372) ships *cumulative* chunks `1..N.out` per round and
stamps the assembled segment's `shipoutPage` with `maxShipout`, the
debug-toast text for a stamped pdf-segment should make the
concatenation visible: a `shipoutPage=N>1` segment shows
`[1..N.out] ${bytes} bytes` (the range form), `shipoutPage===1`
remains `[1.out] ${bytes} bytes` (no range needed), and unstamped
segments (`shipoutPage` 0/undefined — legacy / pre-M22.4b
sentinel) stay as `${bytes} bytes`. Independent, well-scoped, no
runtime-protocol changes; product behaviour is unchanged. Lands the
last open piece of priority #1 short of the deferred
bootstrap-cascade gold case.

## Approach

One source edit + two test edits. The format predicate is a
two-branch decision on `shipoutPage`:

- `> 1` → `[1..${n}.out] ${bytes} bytes`
- `=== 1` → `[1.out] ${bytes} bytes`
- otherwise → `${bytes} bytes`

`lastPage` deliberately not surfaced in the toast — the FE
placeholder slot is the user-visible signal for "more pages on
their way", and `flyctl logs` already carries
`daemon-round-done.lastPageReached`. Adding `lastPage` to the toast
would just clutter the high-frequency burst aggregate.

Touch points:

1. `apps/web/src/lib/debugToasts.ts` — pdf-segment branch swap.
2. `apps/web/test/wsClientDebugEvents.test.mjs` — Case 7's
   `[4.out] 1024 bytes` becomes `[1..4.out] 1024 bytes`; new
   sub-case pins `shipoutPage===1 → [1.out] ${bytes} bytes`.
3. `apps/web/test/compileCycleTracker.test.mjs` — Case 5's
   `"1.0s — [2.out] 1234 bytes"` becomes
   `"1.0s — [1..2.out] 1234 bytes"`.
