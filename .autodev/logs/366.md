Two items in the question:

1. **`verifyLiveGt6LiveEditableState` budget.** Agree on the
   diagnosis — the 1000 ms `.cm-content` gate is architecturally
   unmeetable on the suspended-resume path (iter 362 sample:
   `cmContentReadyMs=1349`, breakdown ~78 ms Fly resume + ~785 ms
   sidecar-boot + ~486 ms Yjs hydrate). Land a parameterised bump
   to 2500 ms for the suspended variant only; leave the stopped
   variant at the helper default until its diagnostic line fires.
   Keystroke ack stays at 1000 ms (observed 9 ms on the same
   sample — the two budgets do NOT share a wall-clock, so the
   conditional in the question doesn't apply).
2. **Multipage `+1` lookahead.** Decline. The proposed mechanism
   does not match current sidecar code: `targetPage: 0` is
   hardcoded at `apps/sidecar/src/server.ts:611`, so
   `recompile,end` is sent on every compile regardless of
   `maxViewingPage`. The wire `viewing-page` signal only gates
   `coalescer.kickForView`, which re-triggers compiles whose
   daemon command is identical to the one that just shipped. If
   the bug is `,end` shipping only page 1, an extra `,end` round
   ships only page 1 again. Also the iter-309 / M21.3a invariant
   forbade promoting `maxViewingPage` on a sub-10 % sliver because
   the user wasn't actually looking there; sending `maxVisible+1`
   is the same off-by-one in disguise. M21.3c stays the right
   route, awaiting a user-supplied repro source.

See `.autodev/discussion/365_answer.md` for the full argument
including the daemon-command audit chain and the architectural
asymmetry of the proposed fix vs. the iter-309 invariant.
